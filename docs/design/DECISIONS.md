| — | Trojian housing: Compounds | (Eli, 2026-07-13) **Housing is organized into Compounds, not detached houses.** A Compound = one large walled lot: a courtyard/atrium farm at the center (crops for the resident families) surrounded by condo-like apartments or mansions belonging to owning families. Built up multiple stories where land is scarce; the ROOFTOP of a tall compound is walled off and rented out as mass low-income housing (tents/mud huts — informal, flammable, cheap). Wealth gradient IN MATERIALS: mansions/condos of owning families = **Reman engineering** (concrete-equivalent — Rema is the canon engineer-civilization); rooftop slum housing = cheap cloth/mudbrick/tent construction. Visual touchstone Eli gave: "the big crab in Ald-Ruhn" (Morrowind) — one large architectural shell containing a whole community's housing stratified by income, not a suburb of identical single-family lots. **Applies retroactively**: the "12 homes" of the city-block population effort become ~12 dwelling UNITS within one compound (a few condo/mansion units + several rooftop tent/hut units), not 12 separate buildings. **"Skyrunners"**: Granadad's in-world term for burglars/assassins, named because they break in through compound ceilings and flee across the rooftop-slum layer — a natural third `Job.Villain.*` alongside Robber/Cutpurse, tied to the Skyrunning skill and the Persona cover-identity seam. **Rooftops are socially unseemly** — being seen on one marks a Trojian as low-status or criminal; this is *why* the poor live there and *why* it's a villain highway. Retroactively explains BLESSING-QUEUE #6 (Skylining/Skyrunning kept Favored "for the Wielder") — Gabri's maxed social immunity exempts him alone from that stigma. Binding: no future rooftop-social mechanic may penalize a presented-Wielder actor. |

| — | Crime/Arrest/Escalation (supersedes ACTORS-SPEC.md §2.7 item 5 for Skyrunners) | Eli re-ruled 2026-07-14 (verbatim): "guards should arrest criminals for 1-3 days before turning them loose unless they're a skyrunner then it's cut off their hand first offense and hanging on the second." This **supersedes, for Skyrunners specifically**, ACTORS-SPEC.md's earlier §2.7/item-5 ruling "the Watch arrests, never executes" — that hurt-not-kill rule stands unchanged for ordinary Robbers/Cutpurses (arrest → `HELD` → a drawn 1-3 day sentence → release, no execution path exists for them), but a Skyrunner's 2nd repeat offense is now a permanent `EXECUTED` status (hanged; the actor is never removed from `ActorRegistry` — no removal path exists — it stays resident, permanently inert).
